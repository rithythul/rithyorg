## CRITICAL Iran missile report sends crypto markets sharply lower

The largest cryptocurrencies reversed sharply after Iran's Fars news agency claimed two missiles hit a U.S. warship, with oil spiking 5% before the U.S. denied the report. Bitcoin fell from a $80,594 high to $79,000, while Ethereum, Solana, and Dogecoin all experienced significant declines. The incident highlights how geopolitical tensions continue to create extreme volatility in crypto markets, with traders reacting rapidly to unconfirmed reports. This pattern of military-related news driving crypto dumps has become increasingly common in 2026.

## IMPORTANT Lawyer seeks to seize 30,765 ETH from Arbitrum DAO

A lawyer has appeared on Arbitrum DAO forums seeking funds for victims of decades-old North Korean terrorist acts, aiming to seize 30,765 ETH frozen after last month's rsETH exploit. The motion cites alleged links between the attack and DPRK-linked hacking groups such as Lazarus, and invokes a New York restraining notice that could block Arbitrum from releasing the funds. This case raises critical questions about DAO governance, legal responsibility for hacks, and the complex interplay between traditional legal systems and decentralized autonomous organizations.
