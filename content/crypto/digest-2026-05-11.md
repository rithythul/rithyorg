# Senate to Vote on Major Crypto Bill May 14 as Swiss BTC Reserve Push Fails — May 11, 2026

The **Senate Banking Committee will vote on the long-awaited comprehensive crypto legislation** on May 14, the most significant US crypto regulatory vote this year. The markup includes the GENIUS Act and broader market structure provisions that could establish the first comprehensive federal framework for digital assets. An attorney analysis suggests the CLARITY Act provisions could bring crypto firms back to the US if passed. A new poll shows 52% public support for the CLARITY Act, with 70% saying the US should have already passed crypto legislation.

Why it matters: This single vote could end years of regulatory uncertainty and determine whether the US remains competitive in the global crypto industry.

Sources: [Reuters](https://reuters.com), [CNBC](https://cnbc.com), [Bitcoin News](https://bitcoinnews.com)

### Swiss Central Bank Bitcoin Reserve Initiative Fails

The **grassroots push to force the Swiss National Bank (SNB) to hold bitcoin on its balance sheet failed** after falling short of the required 100,000 signatures. The initiative would have mandated the SNB to allocate a portion of its reserves to BTC, following a growing global trend of pro-BTC sentiment among central bank watchers. Switzerland had been seen as one of the most promising jurisdictions for a central bank BTC reserve due to its crypto-friendly regulatory environment.

Why it matters: Central bank BTC adoption faces significant institutional resistance even in the most crypto-friendly countries. The failure delays the narrative of sovereign BTC reserves for now.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://coindesk.com)

## IMPORTANT

### Layerzero RPC Poisoning Linked to $292M KelpDAO Hack

Layerzero disclosed an **RPC poisoning incident linked to the $292 million KelpDAO hack**, one of the largest DeFi exploits of 2026. The attack involved compromising remote procedure call endpoints that KelpDAO relied on for interacting with Ethereum, allowing attackers to manipulate transaction data and drain funds. This was not a smart contract vulnerability but an infrastructure-level compromise, raising broader concerns about RPC endpoint security across DeFi.

Why it matters: A $292M exploit through infrastructure compromise rather than smart contract flaws represents a new threat vector that many protocols may not be prepared for.

Source: [Bitcoin News](https://bitcoinnews.com)

### Morgan Stanley BTC ETF Posts Zero Outflows in First Month

**Morgan Stanley's MSBT**, the first spot bitcoin ETF offered by a major Wall Street bank, has recorded **zero outflows since launch** and drew approximately $194 million in its first month. Frank Chaparro confirmed the strong debut, while separate data shows MSBT ended its first trading month with no redemptions. The absence of outflows during the first month is notable given the typical volatility of new ETF products.

Why it matters: A major Wall Street bank's BTC ETF holding steady demand with zero redemptions signals that traditional finance institutions and their clients are building long-term BTC positions, not trading in and out.

Sources: [Bloomberg](https://bloomberg.com), [bloomingbit](https://bloomingbit.com), [MEXC](https://mexc.com), [Traders Union](https://tradersunion.com)

### US Spot BTC ETFs Record $3.4B Inflow Over Six Consecutive Weeks

US spot bitcoin ETFs have recorded a combined **$3.4 billion in inflows over six consecutive weeks**, the longest positive streak in nine months. The sustained demand comes amid legislative progress and growing institutional adoption, with Bitcoin trading above the $80K milestone.

Why it matters: Six straight weeks of inflows represents the longest stretch of institutional demand since the initial BTC ETF launch frenzy, suggesting structural buying pressure rather than speculative positioning.

Source: [BeInCrypto](https://beincrypto.com)

### Bitcoin Mining Giants Back Stratum V2 as Costs Rise

Major **bitcoin mining companies are backing the adoption of Stratum V2**, the next-generation mining protocol, as operational costs continue to rise. The protocol upgrade aims to improve mining efficiency, security, and decentralization by allowing miners to construct their own block templates rather than relying on mining pool operators.

Why it matters: Stratum V2 adoption by major miners could significantly improve Bitcoin's decentralization and censorship resistance at the protocol level.

Source: [crypto.news](https://crypto.news)
