## CRITICAL Brazil Bans Crypto for Cross-Border Payments
Brazil's central bank banned stablecoins and crypto from cross-border settlement. Fintechs and payment firms can't use crypto rails anymore. Individual holders are unaffected — but the actual payment infrastructure just got shut down in LatAm's largest economy. Watch for copycat regulation across emerging markets.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/05/02/brazil-s-central-bank-bans-stablecoin-and-crypto-settlement-in-cross-border-payments)

## CRITICAL $292M DeFi Hack — Biggest of 2026
Multi-chain flash loan exploit drained $292M from a lending protocol. Auditors missed it entirely. Happening the same week Wall Street announced new onchain strategies makes the timing brutal. The gap between institutional interest and DeFi security isn't closing — it's widening.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/02/the-usd292m-crypto-hack-exposed-defi-s-weak-spots-here-s-what-must-change-insiders-say)

## IMPORTANT CLARITY Act Clears Senate Yield Hurdle
Crypto industry backed a yield compromise in the CLARITY Act. Stablecoin firms need to restructure from "buy and hold" to "buy and use" reward models. Crypto Council for Innovation pushed back on the broad prohibition scope, but Senate Banking is moving toward markup. Bitcoin climbed above $78K alongside a new S&P 500 record.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2026/05/02/crypto-industry-backs-clarity-act-yield-compromise-pushes-senate-banking-for-markup)

## IMPORTANT Bitcoin eCash Fork Draws Developer Warnings
Paul Sztorc's eCash fork proposal for Bitcoin is getting pushback. Developers call the airdrop-based approach "hazardous" — citing user risk, uneven distribution, and philosophical concerns. Classic tension between Bitcoin innovation and network conservatism.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/05/02/bitcoin-s-hazardous-airdrop-why-developers-are-warning-against-paul-sztorc-s-ecash-fork)

## POSITIVE Prediction Markets Hit $240B Industry
Prediction markets grew to $240B, driven by retail users trading crypto, politics, and more. Bitget and Polymarket data shows the space is shedding its "casino" reputation — becoming a mainstream tool for tracking news and events. Same arc as traditional betting markets becoming legitimate financial instruments.

Source: [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/05/02/prediction-markets-are-ditching-the-casino-label-to-become-a-regular-part-of-how-people-track-the-news)
