# Crypto Daily Digest — May 5, 2026

## 1. Bitcoin Breaks $80,000 as CLARITY Act Approaches Finish Line

Bitcoin surged past $80,000 for the first time since January, reaching a three-month high. The rally was catalyzed by bipartisan compromise on the CLARITY Act — the long-awaited US crypto regulatory framework that is now approaching a final vote in Congress.

The breakout triggered massive liquidations, wiping out **$301 million in short positions** as bears were caught offside. Crypto stocks rallied in tandem, with Coinbase, Riot Platforms, and Marathon Digital all posting significant gains.

**Sources:** Fortune, Bloomberg, Investor's Business Daily, Binance

**Why it matters:** The CLARITY Act represents the most significant US crypto legislation to date. Passage would provide regulatory clarity that institutional investors have been demanding, potentially unlocking hundreds of billions in institutional capital.

## 2. Global Crackdown: 276 Arrested, $701 Million Seized

International law enforcement agencies conducted a coordinated crackdown on crypto fraud, shutting down **9 scam centers**, arresting **276 suspects**, and seizing **$701 million** in assets. The operation targeted sophisticated crypto-based Ponzi schemes and investment fraud rings operating across multiple jurisdictions.

In a separate incident, the former CEO of a Polish cryptocurrency trading platform disappeared with **4,500 Bitcoin private keys** after the exchange was exposed as a Ponzi scheme.

**Sources:** The Hacker News, WEEX

**Why it matters:** Despite regulatory crackdowns, crypto fraud continues at massive scale. The $701M seizure represents one of the largest single anti-fraud operations in crypto history.

## 3. Trump's World Liberty Financial Sues Justin Sun for Defamation

The Trump family's crypto venture, World Liberty Financial, has filed a defamation lawsuit against Tron founder and crypto billionaire Justin Sun. The legal action escalates existing tensions between two of the cryptocurrency industry's most politically connected figures.

**Source:** CBS News

**Why it matters:** Both parties have significant influence in crypto policy circles. The lawsuit could impact regulatory dynamics and reshape alliances within the industry.

## 4. BlackRock's European Bitcoin ETP Crosses $1.1 Billion

BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust ETP (IB1T) in Europe has surpassed **$1.1 billion in assets under management**, now holding approximately **14,200 BTC**. The milestone signals continued institutional appetite for Bitcoin exposure through regulated European products.

**Source:** Coinpedia

**Why it matters:** BlackRock's growing European presence complements its dominant US spot ETF (IBIT), creating a global institutional Bitcoin infrastructure.

## 5. Ethereum Whales Accumulate $322M as Glamsterdam Upgrade Nears

Ethereum whales have accumulated **$322 million in ETH**, while Tom Lee of Fundstrat declared that "crypto spring" has begun. The largest Ethereum treasury bought an additional **$238 million in ether**.

**Sources:** CoinDesk, The Defiant, openPR

**Why it matters:** Large-scale ETH accumulation ahead of a major network upgrade suggests institutional confidence in Ethereum's trajectory. Glamsterdam's capacity improvements could drive renewed DeFi activity.
